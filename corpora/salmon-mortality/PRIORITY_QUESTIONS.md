# Priority Questions: Salmon Mortality

---

## Q1: Magnitude and trends in production mortality

**Question**
What are the documented levels and trends in production mortality in
Norwegian Atlantic salmon farming, and what causes account for the
largest shares of total mortality?

**Why this question matters**
Total production mortality is a headline welfare and production indicator.
Understanding its composition — how much is attributable to disease,
handling, lice, environmental conditions, and unknown causes — is
necessary for both welfare regulation and for contextualising lice-specific
mortality estimates.

**Expected disagreement**
Official statistics (Fiskeridirektoratet) provide aggregate figures,
but attribution to specific causes is contested. Industry reporting
incentives may affect what is recorded as cause of death.

**Evidence types needed**
`empirical_observational`, `regulatory`, `review`

**Key metadata filters**
`relevance: production_mortality, cause_of_death, trends`

## Q2: Production stage and mortality risk

**Question**
How does mortality risk vary across production stages (smolt, post-smolt,
grow-out, pre-slaughter), and what factors explain stage-specific patterns?

**Why this question matters**
Mortality is not evenly distributed across the production cycle. Certain
stages — particularly the smolt transfer period and thermal treatment
windows — carry disproportionate risk. Stage-specific evidence is needed
for targeted welfare improvement and risk management.

**Expected disagreement**
Data at production-stage level is limited in public literature. Most
observational studies use aggregate farm-level data.

**Evidence types needed**
`empirical_observational`, `empirical_experimental`

**Key metadata filters**
`production_stage: smolt, post_smolt, grow_out, pre_slaughter`

## Q3: Disease-related mortality

**Question**
What is the evidence on mortality attributable to the most significant
infectious diseases in Norwegian salmon farming (including PD, IHN,
AGD, heart conditions), and how have disease patterns changed over time?

**Why this question matters**
Disease is a major and growing driver of mortality. Understanding disease
dynamics — including interactions with lice management — is necessary for
a complete picture of production mortality.

**Expected disagreement**
Disease attribution is methodologically difficult. Mortality from multiple
concurrent conditions is common. Veterinary reporting data and research
studies may give different pictures.

**Evidence types needed**
`empirical_observational`, `review`, `institute_report`

**Key metadata filters**
`relevance: disease, PD, IHN, AGD, cardiac, pathogen`

## Q4: System type and mortality outcomes

**Question**
How do different production systems (open cage, semi-closed, RAS) compare
on overall mortality rates and mortality cause profiles?

**Why this question matters**
Technology transitions in the industry are partly motivated by welfare
and mortality arguments. The evidence base for claims that alternative
systems improve mortality outcomes is limited and context-dependent.

**Expected disagreement**
Emerging system technologies have limited long-term data. Early results
may not reflect mature operational performance. Industry proponents and
critical researchers often interpret the same limited dataset differently.

**Evidence types needed**
`empirical_observational`, `review`, `white_paper`

**Key metadata filters**
`system_type: open_cage, semi_closed, ras, closed_containment`
