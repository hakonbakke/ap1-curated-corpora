# Corpus: Salmon Mortality

## Scope

This corpus covers evidence on **production mortality in farmed Atlantic
salmon from all causes** — including disease, biological factors, handling
stress, environmental conditions, and systemic production failures.

It provides the broader production context within which lice-specific
mortality should be understood.

---

## Why this question matters

Norway has experienced persistently high and in some periods increasing
mortality rates in salmon production — a trend documented by
Riksrevisjonen (2023), Veterinærinstituttet, and Havforskningsinstituttet.
Mortality rates have in recent years averaged 15–20% annually across
the production cycle.

This has consequences for:
- **Animal welfare** — high mortality is a primary welfare indicator
- **Production economics** — mortality represents direct loss of value
- **Regulatory legitimacy** — industry's social licence depends in part
  on demonstrating animal welfare responsibility
- **Attribution** — understanding how lice-related mortality relates to
  total mortality requires a full picture of all mortality causes

This corpus does not duplicate the lice-specific corpora. It focuses on
systemic and multi-causal mortality patterns, providing context for
interpreting lice-specific findings.

## Priority questions
