# Corpus: Salmon Lice and Mortality of Wild Salmonids

## Scope

This corpus covers evidence on **sea lice transmission from salmon farms to
wild salmonids**, and the effects of lice infestation on wild salmon and
sea trout survival.

This is the primary evidence base underpinning the Norwegian **traffic light
system** (trafikklyssystemet) for production capacity regulation.

---

## Why this question matters

Sea lice from farms are the most politically prominent pathway of impact
from aquaculture on wild salmonid populations. The threshold values used
in the traffic light system — determining whether production areas are
allowed to grow, maintain, or reduce capacity — are derived from models
of lice-induced smolt mortality.

The evidence is both rich and contested. Model-based estimates (primarily
from Havforskningsinstituttet) suggest substantial mortality in several
production areas. Field-based studies show more variable effects.
The regulatory thresholds themselves involve precautionary judgements that
go beyond what the empirical literature directly supports.

This corpus makes that complexity visible and traceable.

---

## Priority questions

See `PRIORITY_QUESTIONS.md` for the full list.

Core questions:
1. What is the evidence on lice-induced mortality in wild salmonid smolts,
   and how does it vary by production area, season, and methodology?
2. What are the scientific foundations of the threshold values in the
   traffic light system, and where do model assumptions depart from
   empirical validation?
