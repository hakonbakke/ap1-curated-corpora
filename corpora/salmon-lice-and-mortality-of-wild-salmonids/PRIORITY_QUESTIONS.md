# Priority Questions: Salmon Lice and Mortality of Wild Salmonids

These questions guide corpus construction and analysis for this corpus.
Documents are included only if they contribute to answering at least one
of these questions.

---

## Q1: Lice-induced smolt mortality — magnitude and variability

**Question**
What is the observed and modelled range of sea lice-induced mortality
in wild Atlantic salmon and sea trout smolts, and how does this vary
by production area, season, farm density, and methodology?

**Why this question matters**
This is the core empirical question behind the traffic light system.
Mortality estimates vary substantially across studies. Understanding
the sources of that variation — methodology, geography, temporal coverage
— is necessary to interpret the regulatory thresholds critically.

**Expected disagreement**
Model-based studies (primarily HI) tend to produce higher mortality
estimates than field-based studies. There is ongoing debate about
whether models adequately account for compensatory mortality, smolt
condition, and river-specific population dynamics.

**Evidence types needed**
`empirical_observational`, `empirical_experimental`, `model`, `review`

**Key metadata filters**
`production_stage: wild`, `relevance: lice, smolt_mortality, wild_salmon`

## Q2: Scientific basis of traffic light system thresholds

**Question**
On what scientific evidence are the threshold values in the Norwegian
traffic light system based, and where do the underlying model assumptions
depart from empirical validation?

**Why this question matters**
The traffic light system directly determines production capacity for
Norwegian salmon farming. The thresholds are regulatory choices, not
pure empirical findings. Making the scientific basis — and its limitations
— visible is essential for evidence-informed debate.

**Expected disagreement**
HI defends the model basis of the thresholds. Industry representatives
challenge the models' assumptions and their validation against field data.
There are also legitimate academic debates about the models' sensitivity
to parameter choices.

**Evidence types needed**
`model`, `regulatory`, `review`, `empirical_observational`

**Key metadata filters**
`relevance: trafikklys, threshold, lice_model, population_model`

## Q3: Wild salmonid population responses at river level

**Question**
What evidence exists for population-level effects of sea lice pressure
on individual wild salmon rivers, and how are these effects detected
and attributed?

**Why this question matters**
Aggregate mortality estimates do not map directly onto impacts on
specific rivers or populations. Understanding how effects are detected
at river level — and the methodological challenges in attribution —
is important for interpreting both the science and the regulatory logic.

**Expected disagreement**
Attribution of population changes to lice (vs. other stressors like
climate, habitat loss, ocean conditions) is methodologically difficult.
Studies differ substantially in their causal claims.

**Evidence types needed**
`empirical_observational`, `model`, `review`

**Key metadata filters**
`production_stage: wild`, `relevance: population_effects, river, attribution`

## Q4: Geographic and seasonal variation in lice exposure

**Question**
How does the spatial and temporal pattern of lice exposure vary across
Norwegian production areas, and what factors drive this variation?

**Why this question matters**
Lice impact is not uniform. Production area, fjord geometry, temperature,
current patterns, and farm density all affect exposure. A system that
treats all areas uniformly may miss the most important drivers.

**Expected disagreement**
Relatively less contested empirically, but model predictions and field
observations sometimes diverge on specific production areas.

**Evidence types needed**
`empirical_observational`, `model`

**Key metadata filters**
`geography: PO1–PO13`, `relevance: lice_distribution, exposure, spatial`
