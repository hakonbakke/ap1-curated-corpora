# Summary: Fjelldal et al. (2020)

**Full title:** Effects of laboratory salmon louse infection on osmoregulation, growth and survival in Atlantic salmon  
**Journal:** Conservation Physiology, 8: coaa023 (Open Access, CC BY 4.0)  
**Institution:** IMR Matre; River Etne F1 wild broodstock  
**Priority questions:** Q6, Q7

---

## What this paper does

28-day controlled *L. salmonis* challenge on **~40 g** post-smolts (9°C, 34 ppt) to define **lice g⁻¹** thresholds for osmoregulatory failure relevant to Norwegian risk index/TLS.

## Experimental summary

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|--------|
| Copepodids added | 4290 (~26 fish⁻¹) |
| Attachment | ~69% of added copepodids |
| Day 30 intensity | **0.38** mobile lice g⁻¹ (0.07–0.9); ~17 lice fish⁻¹ |
| SGR | **0.42** vs **0.95** % day⁻¹ (infected vs control) |
| Mortality | 23.6% infected vs 12.7% control* |

*Control losses partly Vibrio in two tanks (days 15–22).

## Physiological thresholds (normal infected fish, n=35)

| Endpoint | Threshold (lice g⁻¹) |
|----------|---------------------|
| Plasma Cl⁻ | **0.18** |
| Plasma Na⁺ | **0.22** |
| Moribund onset | **>0.2** (extreme ions/cortisol) |

Infected vs control plasma (normal fish): Na⁺ 190.6 vs 169.3 mmol L⁻¹; Cl⁻ 167.6 vs 140.3; cortisol 193.8 vs 86.4 ng ml⁻¹.

## Correlations (infected)

- Infection intensity vs Cl⁻ **R²=0.47**; vs SGR **R²=0.36** (negative).
- SGR vs Na⁺, Cl⁻, osmolality, cortisol **R²=0.48–0.65**.

## Policy link

Validates intensity-based monitoring (NALO 2019: 31% of 2501 post-smolts infected; regional means 0.06–0.67 lice g⁻¹) and Taranger/Kristoffersen mortality steps for **~20–40 g** fish.

## Limitations

Lab; outbred lice strain; single dose; mobile stages only.
