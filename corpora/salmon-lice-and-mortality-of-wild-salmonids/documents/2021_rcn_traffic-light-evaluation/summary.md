## What this report does

At the request of the **Norwegian Ministry of Trade, Industry and Fisheries**, the Research Council convened an **international Evaluation Committee (EvalComm)** of **eight scientists** from **five European countries** (chair: **Crawford Revie**, Strathclyde) to assess the **scientific basis** of the **Traffic Light System (TLS)** for salmon aquaculture growth regulation.

**Process:** Constituted late **2020**; **14** online meetings through Dec **2021**; **Interim Report** July **2021**; reviewed **~100 documents**, **>1,000 pages**; engaged Norwegian **Expert Group (ExpGrp)** and **Steering Group**.

**Scope limitation:** **Sea trout** and **Arctic char** assessments **excluded** (Finstad et al. sea-trout method cited as 2019 appendix only).

## System architecture (Figure 1 — key insight)

The EvalComm produced a flow-chart of the TLS impact assessment. Central finding:

> **Taranger et al. (2011) mortality thresholds** (orange oval) feed **all** sea lice impact indexes and create the **strongest dependence** among the **seven complementary assessment endpoints** before the final green/yellow/red classification.

Other elements:

## Main findings (six review themes)

| Section | Finding |
|---------|---------|
| **3.1 Knowledge inclusion** | Ambition good; documentation of outreach and grey-literature rules weak |
| **3.2 Mortality thresholds** | **No solid empirical basis** demonstrated; unchanged since TLS start; **high sensitivity** of outputs to threshold assumptions |
| **3.3 Modelling** | Advanced but **calibration-limited**; recommend mapping sensitivity over endless model refinement (**R7**) |
| **3.4–3.5 Uncertainty** | Under-quantified and under-communicated; use **confidence intervals** and **IPCC-style** language (**R8–R10**) |
| **3.6 Expert elicitation** | Essential to final PA colour but **not** documented to elicitation best practice (**R11**) |
| **5 System performance** | **No assessment** of whether TLS achieves conservation outcomes |

**Overall tone:** TLS is **"probably the most sophisticated salmon risk assessment in operation around the globe"** — while identifying major structural weaknesses.

---

## 15 Recommendations (abbreviated)

| ID | Topic |
|----|-------|
| **R1–R2** | Document knowledge-inclusion; explicit valid-source policy |
| **R3** | Broaden ExpGrp (epistemology, science communication) |
| **R4** | Present TLS as **precautionary + quantitative**, not purely mechanistic |
| **R5–R6** | **Reassess mortality thresholds** regularly; fund **in situ** wild salmon lice studies |
| **R7** | Prioritise **sensitivity analysis** over model refinement |
| **R8–R10** | Quantify uncertainty; **IPCC-style** public communication |
| **R11** | Formal expert-elicitation standards |
| **R12–R13** | Critique monitoring redesigns; more **river stock assessments** |
| **R14** | **External validation** of TLS vs river/conservation outcomes |
| **R15** | Embed TLS in iterative **DPSIR** performance loop |

## Conflict of interest (from report)

Committee members are **international academics** appointed by the **Research Council** at **government request** — independent of Norwegian ExpGrp/SG operations. No aquaculture industry representation on EvalComm listed.

## Relevance to this corpus

Foundational for **Q10**: explains *why* threshold and calibration debates (Johnsen 2021, Jansen 2021, Stige 2022) matter for regulatory legitimacy — not only biological effect size.

Cited by later critiques (**Van Nes et al. 2024**, **Vannes 2024**).

## Limitations

- Not exhaustive technical audit of every model line
- Did not evaluate alternative simpler regulatory frameworks
- Sea trout / char omitted despite ongoing policy intent to expand TLS
