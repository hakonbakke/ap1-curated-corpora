# Summary: Stige et al. (2025)

**Full title:** Comment on "Salmon Lice Biology, Environmental Factors, and Smolt Behaviour With Implications for the Norwegian Salmon Farming Management System: A Critical Review"
**Journal:** Reviews in Aquaculture, 17, e70040
**DOI:** 10.1111/raq.70040
**Document type:** Letter / commentary (open access)
**Priority questions:** Q5, Q7, Q8, Q10

---

## What this paper does

Nine current members of the Norwegian Traffic Light System (TLS) **Expert Group** respond to Van Nes, Imsland & Jones (2024), which argued the TLS systematically overestimates lice-induced mortality on wild post-smolts. The comment accepts many uncertainties but contends the review is one-sided and misrepresents EG methodology. A technical **Appendix A** extends the rebuttal (trawling, sentinel cages, salinity, Allee effect, mortality thresholds, release experiments).

---

## Main arguments

1. **Smolt migration timing** — Five acoustic-telemetry river-years cannot overturn a coast-wide model using 343+ river-years (Vollset et al. 2021); EG does not use a fixed 28-day coastal residence — virtual post-smolt models imply shorter residence for most rivers.

2. **Farm larvae production** — Even if larval production were overestimated, models **calibrate** infestation pressure to observed smolt lice; halving larvae would double the calibration factor, leaving mortality estimates largely unchanged.

3. **One-sided bias narrative** — Review emphasises overestimation pathways but not underestimation (e.g. trawl covers only inner fjord; sentinel cages ~10× lower than trawl per Stige et al. 2022). Assessment is **expert elicitation**, not an average of seven indices.

4. **Sea trout index** — "Poor correlation" claim mis cites Vollset et al. (2017): r = **0.71** between trout-derived index and log salmon smolt abundance.

5. **Post-attachment lice loss** — EG uses ~40% correction (chalimus→pre-adult); experimental losses span 0–80%; handling loss before counts biases the other way.

6. **Mortality thresholds** — Grimnes & Jakobsen (1996) could not test tolerance at adult louse stage; field data (Holst et al.) support threshold **<10** mobile lice, not ~30 as review suggests.

7. **Release experiments & drugs** — Lice resistance to emamectin benzoate (Vollset et al. 2023) and treatment toxicity (Lennox et al. 2020) omitted — both could bias toward **underestimating** lice mortality.

8. **Conflicts of interest** — Van Nes et al. did not disclose Salmon Tracking 2030 industry funding or first author's Nova Sea board role.
