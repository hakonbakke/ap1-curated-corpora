# Van Nes, Imsland & Jones 2025 — Response to Stige et al. "Comment on Van Nes, Imsland and Jones"

## Background
This letter responds to the Stige et al. (2025) comment, which argued the Van Nes et al. (2024) review was biased toward evidence suggesting low impact. The authors defend their original findings and respond to the specific criticisms.

## Key Arguments
1. **Migration data**: The EG uses a model based on 25% emigration observations with a fixed 40-day migration period. The acoustic telemetry data cited in the review are more representative of actual migration because they track individual smolts. These data are NOT included in TLS migration estimates.
2. **Calibration compensation**: If both input data (larvae estimates) AND calibration data (lice counts) are unreliable, any compensating effect is negated. Stige et al.'s calibration argument only works if calibration data are themselves reliable.
3. **Lice loss correction**: The EG only recently implemented 40% correction in the NVI model, acknowledging the need. But 5 of 7 endpoints still assume 100% lice survival. The EG's own practice validates the review's claim.
4. **Scientific peer review**: The review underwent extensive peer review with five reviewers. Claims of bias and misunderstanding are "neither scientifically justified nor does it contribute to a constructive scientific debate."
5. **Conflicts of interest**: All relevant conflicts were honestly disclosed. The allegation of hidden conflicts is "a serious and unwarranted allegation."

## Relevance to Corpus
Final piece of the debate chain. Demonstrates the contested nature of TLS methodology and the difficulty of resolving disagreements when both sides have legitimate scientific arguments and institutional positions.
