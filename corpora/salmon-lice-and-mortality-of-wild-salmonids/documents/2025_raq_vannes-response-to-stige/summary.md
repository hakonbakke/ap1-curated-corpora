**Journal:** Reviews in Aquaculture, 17, e70050

**Priority questions:** Q5, Q7, Q8, Q10

---

## What this paper does

The authors of the 2024 TLS critical review reply to the Expert Group's comment (Stige et al. 2025, e70040). They defend the scientific basis and peer-review status of the original review and rebut specific EG claims on migration timing, model calibration, post-attachment lice loss, and conflicts of interest.

---

## Main arguments

1. **Peer review & bias** — Original review underwent extensive peer review (five reviewers); EG allegations of bias and literature misunderstanding are rejected as unconstructive.

2. **Migration timing** — TLS applies modelled timing from **25% emigration** observations with a **fixed 40-day** migration window (EG reports; Vollset et al. 2021). Acoustic telemetry cited in the review shows earlier, shorter migration and is **not** used in TLS mortality assessment — a key uncertainty acknowledged in EG's own reports.

3. **Calibration argument** — Stige et al. claim larvae overestimation is neutralised by calibration to observed smolt lice. Authors counter: if **both** production estimates and calibration observations are unreliable, compensation does not hold; review already discusses calibration uncertainty; Eliasen (2021) noted high variability in calibration data.

4. **Post-attachment loss** — EG's recent **40%** correction in the NVI model acknowledges the problem, but **five of seven** TLS mortality endpoints still assume **100%** survival of attached lice — supporting the review's overestimation claim.

5. **Alignment** — Critique aligns with the independent Evaluation Committee (Eliasen et al. 2021) and uncertainties documented in EG annual reports.

6. **Conflicts of interest** — All relevant interests were disclosed in the review publication process; Stige's allegations are unwarranted.

---

## Relevance to this corpus

Closes the three-paper **Reviews in Aquaculture** exchange on TLS reliability. Critical for representing unresolved scientific disagreement on migration timing, calibration, and mortality translation — core to Q5 and Q10.
