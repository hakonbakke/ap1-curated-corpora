# Summary: Bøhn et al. (2020)

**Full title:** Timing is everything: Survival of Atlantic salmon Salmo salar postsmolts during events of high salmon lice densities
**Journal:** Journal of Applied Ecology, 57: 1149–1160
**DOI:** 10.1111/1365-2664.13612
**Document type:** Research article (four randomized controlled trials)
**Priority questions:** Q4, Q8, Q10

---

## What this paper does

Four large-scale RCTs in River Etne / outer Hardangerfjord (National Salmon Fjord) tested whether migration timing relative to farm-driven lice peaks determines post-smolt survival. **n = 29,817** tagged hatchery-reared postsmolts (Etne broodstock) were released May and June in **2013** (all outer farms fallowed March 2013 → near-zero lice) and **2014** (full production, ~80,000–95,000 t standing biomass). Half received **SubEX** prophylaxis (~16 weeks protection); half sham-treated. Returns were monitored 2014–2017 in a resistance-board weir trap with ~**90%** capture efficiency.

Lice infestation pressure was estimated from **sentinel cages** (14-day total lice per fish, modelled means) and **lakseslus.no** hydrodynamic dispersion (ROC zones: green <1, yellow 1–10, red >10 lice/fish).

## Main findings

### Release design (Table 1)

| Year | Release | Treated n | Control n | Weight (g) |
|------|---------|-----------|-----------|------------|
| 2013 | 18 May | 3,791 | 3,972 | 72±21 |
| 2013 | 9 June | 3,801 | 3,868 | 74±16 |
| 2014 | 18 May | 3,819 | 3,818 | 47±11 |
| 2014 | 9 June | 3,770 | 2,978 | 42±10 |

**559** fish recaptured total (1–4 sea winters).

### Return rates and risk ratios

| Trial | Pattern | RR (95% CI) |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| May 2013 | Controls returned more | 0.52 (0.35–0.76) |
| June 2013 | ~equal | 1.05 (0.81–1.42) |
| May 2014 | ~equal | 1.13 (0.85–1.50) |
| **June 2014** | **Catastrophic control loss** | **55.3 (7.7–398)** |

- **June 2014 control:** 1/2,978 returned (**0.03%**)
- **June 2014 treated:** 70/3,770 returned (**1.86%**)
- Most other groups: **1–2.5%** return (typical marine survival band)

### Lice infestation pressure (sentinel cages)

- May→June increase: **~10×** in 2013 (0.06→0.66 lice/fish/14 d); **~25×** in 2014 (0.37→8.87)
- **2013 fallow** reduced pressure **84–92%** vs 2014 full production
- Lowest pressure consistently at protected inner site Etne

### Statistical model (Table 2, logistic GLM on return)

- Lice Infestation Pressure: **negative** (estimate −2.76, p<0.0001)
- Treatment (prophylaxis): **positive** (0.66, p<0.0001)
- **Lice × treatment interaction** significant (2.71, p<0.0001) — prophylaxis helps at high lice, can hurt at low lice

### Policy context (Norwegian TLS)

Paper cites TLS colour rules: **green** 0–10%, **yellow** 10–30%, **red** >30% lice-induced mortality of wild salmon; June 2014 untreated scenario far exceeds red threshold. Farm biomass context: **386 M** farmed vs **~0.5 M** wild returns (2017).

## Limitations

- Hatchery F1 smolts, not fully wild fish
- Lice exposure inferred from cages/models, not on-route counts on experimental fish
- Cannot separate predation/starvation from lice in absolute mortality — only treatment contrast
- Single intensive fjord system

---

## Place in the literature

Resolves part of the conflicting Norwegian RCT literature: lice effects appear when infestation pressure and migration timing align. Pairs with **Fjelldal et al. (2020)** (physiological thresholds) and TLS modelling papers (**Johnsen et al. 2021**; **Jansen & Gjerde 2021**) as the field-experiment pole of the evidence chain.
