# Summary: Kristoffersen et al. (2018)

**Full title:** Quantitative risk assessment of salmon louse-induced mortality of seaward-migrating post-smolt Atlantic salmon  
**Journal:** Epidemics, 23: 19–33  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.epidem.2017.11.001 (Open Access, CC BY-NC-ND)  
**Authors:** Norwegian Veterinary Institute (+ Uni Research)  
**Priority questions:** Q2–Q5, Q7–Q8, Q10

---

## What this paper does

NVI **Traffic Light System** risk model: farm lice → infestation pressure → sentinel-calibrated exposure → shortest-path migration → **Taranger et al. (2015)** mortality steps for **401 rivers**, **13 production zones**, **2014–2016**.

## Model chain (key parameters)

- Egg production: 300 eggs/female from temperature at 3 m; 126°-day development; 17% daily larval mortality.
- Infestation pressure: kernel to 100 km seaway; calibrated on **7911** fish, **329** sentinel trials (May–June 2013–2016).
- Migration: **20 g** smolts, **8.2 cm s⁻¹**, 40-day window per zone, shortest path to 12 nm.
- Mortality: e.g. 0% (<2 lice), 20% (2–3), 50% (4–6), 100% (>6) — normal scheme.

## Main results

| Finding | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Density dependence | Eggs per biomass varies **8.1×** across zones; LBD correlation **r=0.70** (p=0.008) |
| Sentinel model | log(IP) coef. **0.843**; adj. R² **0.39**; ICC **0.551** |
| Hotspot | **Zone 3** (west) highest infestation; **zone 6** highest proportional mortality 2016 (large smolt potential) |
| Mortality 2016 (zone 3) | Basic **~2–17%** per river; worst-case **~10–72%** |
| Year effect | **2016 > 2014–2015** (treatment efficacy concerns) |

## Uncertainty / sensitivity

Largest leverage: infestation random effects (best/worst case), migration timing (±2–4 weeks), period length (20–80 d), progression speed, mortality scheme. Shortest-path routing and cage-vs-swimming smolts debated.
