# Summary: McCormick et al. (1998)

**Full title:** Movement, migration, and smolting of Atlantic salmon (*Salmo salar*)  
**Journal:** Canadian Journal of Fisheries and Aquatic Sciences, 55(Suppl. 1): 77–92  
**DOI:** 10.1139/f98-011  
**Document type:** Review article (foundational)  
**Priority questions:** Q4

---

## What this paper does

Synthesises fry-to-smolt movements, smoltification physiology/behaviour, and mortality drivers for Atlantic salmon, introducing the **physiological** vs **ecological smolt window** framework.

## Main findings (quantitative highlights)

- **Physiological window:** Photoperiod-driven neuroendocrine cascade (GH, cortisol, thyroid); gill Na⁺,K⁺-ATPase peaks then declines ~80% within **~400 degree-days** after peak—window narrows in warm water; dam delays increase desmolt risk.
- **Ecological window:** Fig. 3 simulation—aligned migration/environment → higher adult returns (~166 vs ~94 when shifted 2 weeks).
- **Migration cues:** Spring warming (often ~10°C) and discharge; River **Imsa** smolt timing **91–95%** explained by April–May temperature (start 5.8–11.2°C).
- **Stressors:** 12 h acid (pH 5.2) can eliminate salinity tolerance; acid rivers ~**10×** lower returns than limed sites; hatchery smolt survival ~**half** wild (Imsa).
- **Corridor:** Mainstems and estuaries most impacted by dams, acid, pollution—where post-smolts are exposed to coastal parasites.
