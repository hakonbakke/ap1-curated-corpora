# Comment on "Salmon Lice Biology, Environmental Factors, and Smolt Behaviour With Implications for the Norwegian Salmon Farming Management System: A Critical Review"

**Document ID:** `2025_raq_stige-comment-vannes`  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1111/raq.70040  
**Priority questions:** Q5, Q7, Q8, Q10  
**Provenance:** Authored by **Norwegian TLS Expert Group** members—institutional stake in defending TLS methodology.

## Key findings

- TLS **Expert Group** response to **Van Nes et al. (2024)**; argues review contains **misreadings** of EG methods and literature.
- **Migration**: claims Van Nes over-weights **5 telemetry points** vs **343+ river-years** model input; disputes **28-day coastal residence** characterisation of VPS models.
- **Larvae / calibration**: argues **calibration** couples pressure to observed counts—**halving larvae** would **rescale** calibration, so larvae bias **need not** bias mortality estimates (disputed in Van Nes et al. 2025 response).
- **One-sidedness**: asserts review ignores **underestimation** pathways (e.g. trawl spatial limits; sentinel cage underestimation).
- **Lice loss / thresholds**: defends **~40%** loss correction and cites experimental survival patterns at high loads; **COI**: flags Van Nes funding/board ties.
