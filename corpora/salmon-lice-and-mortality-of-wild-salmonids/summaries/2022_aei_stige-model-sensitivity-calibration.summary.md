# Modelling salmon lice-induced mortality of wild salmon post-smolts is highly sensitive to calibration data

**Document ID:** `2022_aei_stige-model-sensitivity-calibration`  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.3354/aei00443  
**Priority questions:** Q5, Q7, Q8, Q10

## Key findings

- NVI virtual post-smolt model revised to jointly use **sentinel cage** vs **post-smolt trawl** calibration streams; trawl-based calibration yields roughly **~10× higher infestation rates** than cage-based calibration (after controlling for pressure)—**dominant driver** of mortality differences.
- Sensitivity: **calibration data type (A1) > migration speed (A2)**; with trawl calibration, **density-dependence in infestation (A3)** strongly amplifies differences—helps explain **systematic divergence** among IMR vs NVI vs SINTEF TLS models.
- **Explains disagreement** among official models; **does not resolve** which calibration is closer to “true” wild post-smolt infestation.

## Evidence type

- model / empirical (model–data integration; sensitivity analysis)

## Population-level relevance

- Does not estimate **adult returns**; shows **lice-mortality indices** used in regulation are **highly sensitive** to **monitoring/calibration choices**, affecting inferred **population-level pressure** if those indices feed management.

## Competing or conflicting points

- **Institutional context**: NVI authors; reframes debate with **Jansen & Gjerde (2021)** (VPS vs trawl mortality) and **Van Nes et al. (2024)** (data reliability)—provides mechanism for “models disagree” without endorsing one side’s absolute bias direction.

## Uncertainty / limitations

- Shortest-path migration assumption; 2012–2021 window; trawl genetics assignment has own assumptions.
