# Comment on "Salmon lice-induced mortality of Atlantic salmon post-smolt during migration in Norway" by Johnsen et al. (2021)

**Document ID:** `2021_icesjms_jansen-comment-vps-mortality`  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1093/icesjms/fsab206  
**Priority questions:** Q5, Q7, Q8, Q10

## Key findings

- Compares **VPS-predicted** lice mortality (Johnsen et al. 2021) to **mortality inferred from trawled post-smolts** (NMDC; N=7929; 2017–2020) for **27 area–year** comparisons.
- VPS mortality **higher than trawl-based mortality in 26 of 27** comparisons; mismatch strongest at low mortality; chi-squared on mortality-class distributions **p=0.003**.
- After adjustment for possible further infestation along migration, VPS still higher in **22 of 27** (**p=0.023**); contradicts Johnsen et al. claim that modelled and observed lice levels broadly “coincide.”

## Evidence type

- critique / empirical (reanalysis using published monitoring data)

## Population-level relevance

- Addresses **post-smolt mortality estimates** used upstream of population models; does not itself quantify adult returns, but challenges the **magnitude** of lice mortality feeding into regulatory assessments.

## Competing or conflicting points

- **Direct challenge** to Johnsen et al. (2021) VPS outputs used in TLS-related assessments; aligns with broader **overestimation** critiques (e.g. Van Nes et al. 2024); **Stige et al. (2022)** later explains model divergence partly via **calibration data type** (trawl vs sentinel cage).

## Uncertainty / limitations

- Assumes trawl samples represent inner-fjord migrants; pathway lice accumulation adjustments incomplete; cannot attribute overestimation to single model mechanism.

## Context

- Geography: Norway (fjords aligned to river drainages in comparison)
- Life stage: post-smolt
- System type: coastal migration vs farm-origin lice pressure (implicit)
- Time period: trawl data 2017–2020; comment ties to Johnsen et al. 2021 model period
