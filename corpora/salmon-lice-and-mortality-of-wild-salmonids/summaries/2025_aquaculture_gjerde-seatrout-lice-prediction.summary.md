# Prediction of the salmon lice density on wild sea trout from the mean predicted lice density in the sea: A cross-validation of the data in Myksvoll et al. (2018)

**Document ID:** `2025_aquaculture_gjerde-seatrout-lice-prediction`  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.aquaculture.2025.742149  
**Priority questions:** Q5, Q7, Q10  
**Provenance / COI:** First author **declared witness** for salmon farmers in Norwegian court cases (2020–2021); statistical reanalysis uses **public data**—see `metadata.yaml` `coi_notes`.

## Key findings

- Re-analyses Myksvoll et al. (2018): **5,211** mainly wild sea trout / char at **102** locations; pairs **OMLB** (observed mean lice burden) vs **PMLD** (modelled mean lice density), 2015–2017.
- **Predictive skill weak**: Pearson **r ≈ 0.52–0.55** (R² ≈ **0.28–0.30**); **RPD 1.18–1.19** (“very poor” by conventional RPD<2 rule); **95% CI width ~1 lice/g** vs **0–0.3 lice/g** regulatory band width.
- **TLS colour classification** performance **modest to poor** (e.g. overall accuracy **0.63**; yellow/red especially weak); **sample-area requirements** for reliable green/yellow separation **exceed** sea area of **most** Norwegian production zones per their calculation.

## Evidence type

- empirical / model (statistical cross-validation; methods critique)

## Population-level relevance

- Indirect: if **lice pressure surfaces** used in regulation **do not predict** observed wild infestation, downstream **mortality** and **population** inferences built on those surfaces inherit **structural uncertainty**.

## Competing or conflicting points

- **Direct methodological attack** on particle-model–monitoring link central to TLS; contrasts with **defensive calibration arguments** in Stige et al. (2025) (compensation logic) and **spatial/telemetry** debates in Van Nes vs EG exchange.

## Uncertainty / limitations

- Focuses on **2015–2017** coastal dataset; court-context COI does not change arithmetic of cross-validation but matters for **interpretive weight** in policy settings.

## Context

- Geography: Norway (coastal grid cells)
- Life stage: wild sea trout / some char (monitoring)
- System type: hydrodynamic lice dispersal model outputs vs field sampling
