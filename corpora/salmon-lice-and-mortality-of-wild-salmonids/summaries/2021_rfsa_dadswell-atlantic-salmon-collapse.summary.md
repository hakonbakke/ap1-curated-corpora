# The Decline and Impending Collapse of the Atlantic Salmon (Salmo salar) Population in the North Atlantic Ocean: A Review of Possible Causes

**Document ID:** `2021_rfsa_dadswell-atlantic-salmon-collapse`  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/23308249.2021.1937044  
**Priority questions:** Q9

## Key findings

- Argues **North Atlantic-wide** decline since ~1985 (loss of cyclic returns, low flatlines, smaller adults) **cannot** be fully explained by **climate, lice/aquaculture, prey, or predators** alone as primary drivers.
- Proposes **IUU / high-seas fisheries** (and related offshore exploitation) as a **unifying hypothesis** consistent with spatial/temporal pattern (e.g. collapse signatures far from farms).
- Implies **aquaculture-focused regulation** may **mis-target** the dominant mortality source if the IUU narrative holds—**direct tension** with lice-centric Norwegian management narratives.

## Evidence type

- review (interpretive synthesis; indirect evidence for IUU)

## Population-level relevance

- **Core Q9 position**: challenges the idea that **lice from farms** is the **dominant** explanation for **basin-scale** population collapse; relevant when extrapolating from **fjord-scale** lice studies to **whole-ocean** decline.

## Competing or conflicting points

- **Counterweight** to Norway-centric lice emphasis (Forseth et al. 2017; TLS framing); must be read against **empirical fjord/Norway studies** (e.g. Jansen et al. 2025) that address **regional** returns—not the same spatial claim as basin-wide collapse.

## Uncertainty / limitations

- IUU hypothesis **difficult to test** with available data; limited quantification of IUU mortality; may underweight region-specific aquaculture proximity effects.

## Context

- Geography: North Atlantic (multi-region review)
- Life stage: marine phase / adult returns (population scale)
- System type: mixed fisheries + ocean migration (not farm-specific experiment)
- Time period: emphasis post-1985 patterns; review 2021
