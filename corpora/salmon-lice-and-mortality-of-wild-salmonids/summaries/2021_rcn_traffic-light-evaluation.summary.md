# An evaluation of the Scientific Basis of the Traffic Light System for Norwegian Salmonid Aquaculture

**Document ID:** `2021_rcn_traffic-light-evaluation`  
**Primary link:** https://trafikklyssystemet.no/ (Research Council of Norway evaluation; no DOI in corpus metadata)  
**Priority questions:** Q7, Q8, Q9, Q10

## Key findings

- Independent EvalComm (Research Council of Norway) reviewed extensive TLS documentation; concludes TLS is **sophisticated** but flags **weak empirical basis** for **mortality thresholds** (Taranger et al. 2011/2012) and **lack of reassessment** since inception.
- Three modelling systems (IMR, NVI, SINTEF) are **state-of-the-art** but depend on **variable calibration data**; **uncertainty** is **under-quantified, under-propagated, and under-communicated**.
- **Expert judgment** is central but **poorly documented** vs accepted elicitation standards; **no mechanism** described for assessing TLS **management performance** over time.

## Evidence type

- policy_assessment / expert review

## Population-level relevance

- Evaluates whether **regulatory classifications** (green/yellow/red) rest on **defensible science** for **population-relevant mortality indicators**; does not produce new population estimates but defines **structural weaknesses** in how mortality is inferred.

## Competing or conflicting points

- **Institutional middle ground**: validates seriousness of TLS modelling while endorsing critiques later amplified by Van Nes et al. (2024) and empirical/method papers (Stige et al. 2022; Jansen et al. 2025); cited by both “defence” and “critique” lines in corpus.

## Uncertainty / limitations

- Review-based; dependent on documents available 2017–2021; sea trout/Arctic char not fully integrated in TLS framework at time of review.

## Context

- Geography: Norway (13 production areas)
- Life stage: smolt / post-smolt mortality framing in TLS
- System type: regulatory traffic-light + farm lice reporting + expert models
- Time period: evaluation published 2021
