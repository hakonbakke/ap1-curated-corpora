# A review of marine stressors impacting Atlantic salmon Salmo salar, with an assessment of the major threats to English stocks

**Document ID:** `2022_rfbf_gillson-marine-stressors-salmon`  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1007/s11160-022-09714-x  
**Priority questions:** Q9

## Key findings

- Applies Forseth-style **2D threat classification** to **42 English rivers**: **climate** and **predation** rank as **expanding high-impact**; **salmon mariculture** (lice, escapes, disease) ranked **low impact** for England.
- Attributes low mariculture rank to **minimal farm proximity** to English migration corridors vs Norway/Scotland/BC.
- Notes **marine survival** in NE Atlantic historically low (<5% smolt-to-adult context in review framing)—**multiple stressors** matter at population scale.

## Evidence type

- review (expert classification + literature synthesis)

## Population-level relevance

- **Q9 context**: shows **lice from aquaculture as population threat is geography-dependent**; **not** a claim that lice are harmless globally—**contrast case** to Norwegian prioritisation.

## Competing or conflicting points

- **Direct contrast** to Forseth et al. (2017) Norwegian ranking where lice is top-tier; same framework, different outcome driven by **farm distribution**, not different biology per se.

## Uncertainty / limitations

- England-specific; expert judgment; limited quantitative support for some stressors; not new primary data.

## Context

- Geography: England (four marine plan areas)
- Life stage: marine phase (smolt to adult framing in review)
- System type: predominantly non-aquaculture stressors dominant in classification
- Time period: review ~2022; forward-looking projection discussed in paper
