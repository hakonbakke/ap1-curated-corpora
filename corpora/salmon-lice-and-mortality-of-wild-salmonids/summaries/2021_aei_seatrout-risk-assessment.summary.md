# Development of a risk assessment method for sea trout in coastal areas exploited for aquaculture

**Document ID:** `2021_aei_seatrout-risk-assessment`  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.3354/aei00391  
**Priority questions:** Q1, Q2, Q3, Q5, Q8

## Key findings

- Builds **RML** (reduced marine living area) and **RMT** (reduced marine feeding time) for first-time migrant sea trout using bio-hydrodynamic lice density fields (160×160 m) in PO3 Hardangerfjord and PO5 Romsdalsfjord.
- **Late migration (e.g. June 5)** consistently worse than early; **river-specific** impacts large (e.g. Oselvo ~94–98% RML loss in PO3; Etne 0–68% depending on timing; Eira ~83–87% in PO5).
- Uses critical lice impact thresholds from Taranger et al. (2015) (e.g. 100% impact >30 lice, 50% at 10–30, none <10) combined with ROC calibration vs sentinel cage data (Hardangerfjord 2012–2017).

## Evidence type

- empirical / model (spatial exposure indices + calibration to monitoring)

## Population-level relevance

- Does **not** directly estimate adult returns or population growth rate; uses **habitat/time loss proxies** for coastal sea trout under farm-associated lice pressure—relevant to **population consequences** only via those indicators.

## Competing or conflicting points

- Extends TLS-related modelling ideas to **sea trout**, which spend more time in nearshore lice fields than many salmon smolt migration models emphasise; overlaps thematically with critiques about **which life-history stages** drive impact.

## Uncertainty / limitations

- Thresholds for sea trout from limited lab evidence; fixed 60 g fish; grid resolution less validated than coarser grids; assumes no return to sea after premature freshwater return.

## Context

- Geography: Norway (PO3, PO5)
- Life stage: first-time migrant sea trout (~70 d marine residence scenario)
- System type: coastal fjord + open aquaculture lice dispersal model
- Time period: simulations/calibration centred on 2012–2017 for cages; methods paper 2021
