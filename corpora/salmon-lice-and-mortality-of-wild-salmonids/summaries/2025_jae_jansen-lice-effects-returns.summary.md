# Effects of salmon lice on numbers and size distributions of Atlantic salmon returning to spawn in Norwegian rivers

**Document ID:** `2025_jae_jansen-lice-effects-returns`  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1111/1365-2664.70212  
**Priority questions:** Q4, Q6, Q9, Q10

## Key findings

- Links **NVI model parasite-induced mortality (PIM)** to **104 Norwegian rivers**, **2019–2024** returns (angling + counts); size-structured as **1SW / 2SW / 3SW** with separate PIM effects.
- **1SW (small) returns**: significant **negative** effect of PIM (coefficient **2.883**, **p<0.0005**)—authors state reduced returns **exceed** PIM estimates for this class.
- **2SW**: negative, **not** significant (**p≈0.1**); **3SW**: significant **positive** PIM effect but small numbers—overall pattern interpreted as **net negative** with 1SW dominance.
- **Proportion 1SW** falls from **~60%** (low-PIM rivers) to **<20%** (high-PIM); **~10%** total return reduction predicted at **PIM ~5.5%**; **~10%** spawning biomass reduction at **PIM ~10%**; **>70%** of river-years above the **~5.5%** PIM level for predicted 10% return loss.
- **2024 vs 2019** ~**36%** lower returns—**not** attributed to lice (ocean conditions noted).

## Evidence type

- empirical (observational regression; population-level correlative study)

## Population-level relevance

- **Direct Q9 evidence**: relates **management model mortality metric** to **adult spawner abundance and size structure** at **river population** scale.

## Competing or conflicting points

- **Same first author as Jansen & Gjerde (2021)** (VPS vs trawl overestimation critique); this paper’s results are described as **more supportive** of TLS/NVI mortality signals at **returns** level than that comment implied at **post-smolt** scale; contrasts with **Van Nes et al. (2024)** farm–population decoupling emphasis—**debate axis** is **scale, confounding, and metric choice**.

## Uncertainty / limitations

- Correlation not causation; fixed weight–size mapping; PIM not dominant vs **TS** and **CGW** covariates; independence assumptions across SW classes questioned; variable count quality.

## Context

- Geography: Norway (104 rivers)
- Life stage: returning mature salmon (1SW–3SW)
- System type: regulated aquaculture lice pressure + wild river fisheries/counts
- Time period: 2019–2024 returns; PIM from NVI modelling context
