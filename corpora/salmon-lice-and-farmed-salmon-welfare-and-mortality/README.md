# Corpus: Salmon Lice and Farmed Salmon Welfare and Mortality

## Scope

This corpus covers evidence on the effects of **sea lice infestations and
delousing treatments on the welfare and survival of farmed Atlantic salmon**.

It addresses both the direct welfare impacts of lice (tissue damage, stress,
immune suppression) and the treatment-related mortality and welfare costs
associated with chemical, mechanical, and thermal delousing methods.

---

## Why this question matters

Sea lice management is one of the most operationally significant challenges
in Norwegian salmon farming. Treatment-related mortality has increased as
lice have developed resistance to chemical treatments, driving the adoption
of mechanical and thermal methods with higher welfare costs.

This corpus sits at the intersection of:
- **Fish welfare** regulation and ethical obligations
- **Production economics** (treatment costs and mortality losses)
- **Biosecurity** (treatment efficacy and resistance development)
- **Environmental regulation** (chemical residues, treatment frequency)

The evidence on treatment mortality is also relevant to the wild salmon
corpus: lice load management on farms affects how much lice pressure
reaches wild salmonids in surrounding waters.

---

## Priority questions

See `PRIORITY_QUESTIONS.md` for the full list.

Core questions:
1. What is the observed range of mortality following different delousing
   treatments, and what factors explain this variation?
2. What are the measurable welfare effects of lice infestation and
   delousing stress on farmed Atlantic salmon?
