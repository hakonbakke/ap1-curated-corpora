# Priority Questions: Salmon Lice and Farmed Salmon Welfare and Mortality

---

## Q1: Post-delousing mortality — magnitude and drivers

**Question**
What is the observed range of mortality following different delousing
treatments in farmed Atlantic salmon, and what operational, biological,
and environmental factors drive this variation?

**Why this question matters**
Treatment-related mortality is both a significant welfare concern and
a production risk. Understanding its variability across treatment types,
fish size, temperature, and system type is necessary for evidence-based
treatment guidelines and regulatory standards.

**Expected disagreement**
Estimates vary substantially by treatment type (hydrogen peroxide, thermal,
mechanical, freshwater), fish condition, and operational context. Studies
differ in how they isolate treatment-related mortality from background
mortality. Industry data and research studies may produce different estimates
for the same treatment type.

**Evidence types needed**
`empirical_observational`, `empirical_experimental`

**Key metadata filters**
`relevance: delousing, mortality, treatment`, `production_stage: grow_out`

## Q2: Welfare effects of lice infestation

**Question**
What are the documented welfare effects of sea lice infestation on farmed
Atlantic salmon, and at what infestation levels do welfare indicators
deteriorate significantly?

**Why this question matters**
Regulatory thresholds for treatment are partly based on welfare grounds.
Understanding the relationship between lice burden and welfare indicators
(behaviour, physiology, immune function, wound severity) is necessary
for evaluating whether current thresholds are appropriately protective.

**Expected disagreement**
Welfare effects are difficult to quantify. Studies use different welfare
indicators with varying sensitivity. The relationship between lice count
and welfare impact depends on lice species, life stage, and fish condition.

**Evidence types needed**
`empirical_experimental`, `empirical_observational`, `review`

**Key metadata filters**
`relevance: welfare, lice_burden, stress, immune`, `production_stage: grow_out, post_smolt`

## Q3: Treatment efficacy and resistance development

**Question**
How effective are current delousing methods in reducing lice burdens,
and what is the evidence on resistance development and its operational
consequences?

**Why this question matters**
Declining efficacy of treatments forces escalation to methods with
higher welfare costs. This dynamic is central to understanding both
welfare trends and the increasing mortality burden in the industry.

**Expected disagreement**
Efficacy data is often proprietary. Resistance patterns vary by region
and treatment history. Publicly available data may underrepresent
the current state of resistance.

**Evidence types needed**
`empirical_observational`, `empirical_experimental`, `review`

**Key metadata filters**
`relevance: treatment_efficacy, resistance, delousing`

## Q4: Comparison across treatment methods and systems

**Question**
How do different treatment methods (chemical, thermal, mechanical,
biological) and production systems (open cage, semi-closed) compare
on mortality risk and welfare outcomes?

**Why this question matters**
Treatment choice is both a welfare and an operational decision.
Evidence that systematically compares methods across a common framework
is needed for industry guidance and regulatory standard-setting.

**Expected disagreement**
Comparative evidence is limited. Most studies focus on a single method.
Operational conditions differ substantially across farms, making direct
comparison difficult.

**Evidence types needed**
`empirical_observational`, `empirical_experimental`, `review`

**Key metadata filters**
`relevance: treatment_comparison, system_type`, `system_type: open_cage, semi_closed`
