# Eliasen et al. 2021 — An evaluation of the Scientific Basis of the Traffic Light System for Norwegian Salmonid Aquaculture

## Background
In late 2020, an international Evaluation Committee (EvalComm) of eight scientists from five European countries was appointed by the Norwegian Research Council, at the request of the Ministry of Trade, Industry and Fisheries, to evaluate the scientific basis of the Traffic Light System (TLS) used to regulate Norwegian salmon farming. The TLS classifies 13 production areas as green/yellow/red based on estimated salmon lice-induced mortality of wild salmonids.

## Methods
The EvalComm reviewed over 1,000 pages of documentation from nearly 100 documents. They focused on six key areas: knowledge inclusion, mortality threshold estimation, modelling framework, uncertainty estimation, uncertainty communication, and expert elicitation. They also engaged with members of the Expert Group (ExpGrp) and Steering Group.

## Key Findings
- The TLS is "probably the most sophisticated salmon risk assessment in operation around the globe."
- The mortality thresholds (from Taranger et al. 2011, 2012) lack a solid empirical basis and have not been reassessed since inception — this is a significant weakness.
- The three modelling systems (IMR, NVI, SINTEF) are state-of-the-art but heavily reliant on calibration data with high variability and uncertainty.
- Expert judgment plays a critical but poorly documented role; elicitation does not follow accepted best practice standards.
- Uncertainty is insufficiently quantified, propagated, and communicated.
- There is no mechanism for assessing the actual performance/effectiveness of the TLS as a management system.

## Uncertainty and Limitations
- Calibration data (sentinel cages) show high variability.
- Model outputs may appear precise but rest on uncertain parameters.
- Expert judgment processes are not transparently documented.
- Stock assessment data from rivers used in the TLS is geographically limited.
- Sea trout and Arctic char are not yet included in the TLS framework.

## Relevance to Corpus
This is a foundational governance document — the only independent international peer evaluation of the TLS scientific basis. It identifies structural weaknesses (mortality thresholds, uncertainty communication, expert elicitation) that frame much of the subsequent debate in the literature. Essential for Q7, Q8, Q9, Q10.

## Relation to Other Documents
- Directly evaluates the work of the Expert Group (whose members later authored Stige et al. 2022, 2025).
- Recommendations R5 and R6 (reassess thresholds, conduct in situ studies) are directly relevant to Jansen et al. 2025 and the broader debate.
- Van Nes et al. 2024 cites this evaluation to support their critique.
- Proposes the DPSIR framework for iterative system performance review.
