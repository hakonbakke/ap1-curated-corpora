# Jansen et al. 2025 — Effects of salmon lice on numbers and size distributions of Atlantic salmon returning to spawn in Norwegian rivers

## Background
Negative effects of salmon lice from farms on wild salmonids have been debated for decades. The key question is whether lice infestation from farms reduces returning adult salmon. This study bridges the gap between theoretical post-smolt mortality estimates used in the TLS and actual observations of mature salmon returning to Norwegian rivers, using size-structured population data.

## Methods
Size-structured data on returning mature salmon (small <3kg/1SW, medium 3-7kg/2SW, large >7kg/3SW) from 104 Norwegian rivers over 2019-2024 were compiled from two sources: recreational fishery catches and river counting surveys. Returns were modelled using a log-normal regression as a function of: theoretical smolt production (TS), weighted catches one generation back (CGW), year, a non-linear spatial effect s(geo), and parasite-induced mortality (PIM) from the NVI model. Separate PIM effects were estimated for each size class.

## Key Findings
- Significant negative effect of PIM on returns of 1SW (small) salmon (coefficient 2.883, p<0.0005), meaning reduced returns exceed estimates of PIM.
- Negative but non-significant effect on 2SW salmon (p=0.1).
- Significantly positive effect of PIM on 3SW salmon, but for comparably low numbers — implying overall negative effect.
- Proportion of 1SW salmon decreased from ~60% in low-PIM rivers to <20% in high-PIM rivers.
- A 10% reduction in total returning salmon is predicted at PIM ~5.5%; 10% reduction in spawning weight at PIM ~10%.
- Returning salmon in 2024 were ~36% lower than 2019 (not attributed to lice — likely ocean conditions).
- Over 70% of river-year data exceeds PIM levels where 10% reduction in returns is predicted.

## Uncertainty and Limitations
- Based on correlations — cannot exclude confounding or mediating factors.
- Size-weight relationship assumed (1SW=2kg, 2SW=5kg, 3SW=10kg); some misclassification likely.
- PIM not the dominant covariate — TS and CGW explain more variance.
- Violation of independence assumption (PIM for a given year relates to different SW classes in subsequent years).
- Count data quality varies between rivers, teams, and years.

## Relevance to Corpus
First study linking theoretical post-smolt PIM estimates from the TLS to actual population-level observations of adult salmon returns across 104 rivers. Provides the population-level signal that the Evaluation Committee (Eliasen et al. 2021) called for. Directly addresses Q4, Q6, Q9, Q10.

## Relation to Other Documents
- First author is same as Jansen & Gjerde 2021 (critique of VPS model), but this study supports the TLS mortality estimates at population level.
- Uses NVI model PIM data (Stige et al. 2022, 2023).
- Responds to Evaluation Committee recommendation R6 (in situ effects at population level).
- Explicitly discusses Van Nes et al. 2024 and Jansen & Gjerde 2021 critical studies, noting results are "more in support of" TLS model outcomes.
- References Eliasen et al. 2021 evaluation.
