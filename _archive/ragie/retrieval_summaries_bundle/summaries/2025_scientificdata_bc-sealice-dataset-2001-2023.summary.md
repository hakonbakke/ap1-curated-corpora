# Sea lice infestation dataset for wild and farmed salmon populations on the Pacific coast of Canada (2001–2023)

**Document ID:** `2025_scientificdata_bc-sealice-dataset-2001-2023`  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1038/s41597-025-05653-x  
**Priority questions:** Q1, Q5

## Key findings

- Publishes harmonised **BC** monitoring: **~10,000** farm monthly mean lice records (**~100** farms); **>365,000** wild Pacific salmonid host records across **~17,000** events (2001–2023).
- Documents **protocol drift** (species/stage resolution, reporting granularity, monthly→weekly) reducing strict long-term comparability; notes policy-driven **monitoring declines** post-~2021/2022 in some sub-zones (e.g. Discovery Islands, Broughton).
- Positions dataset as enabling **rigorous large-scale** analysis vs prior **over-generalised** claims from **limited** BC samples (per authors’ framing).

## Population-level relevance

- **No causal attribution** in the descriptor itself; enables future **Q1/Q5** work on **farm–wild infestation coupling** and model validation—**Pacific** context, not Norwegian TLS.

## Competing or conflicting points

- **Methodological mirror** to Norwegian debates: shows why **calibration/observation quality** and **spatial coverage** matter for any RAG answer that leans on “monitoring proves X.”

## Uncertainty / limitations

- Descriptive only; uneven wild sampling; species/regulatory context differs from Atlantic/Norway.

## Context

- Geography: British Columbia, Canada (DFO sub-zones 2.3–3.5)
- Life stage: mixed wild hosts; farmed Atlantic salmon lice reporting
- System type: marine coastal monitoring + aquaculture
- Time period: 2001–2023
