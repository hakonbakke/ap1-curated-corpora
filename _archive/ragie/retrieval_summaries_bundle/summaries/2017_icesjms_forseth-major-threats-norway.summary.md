# The major threats to Atlantic salmon in Norway

**Document ID:** `2017_icesjms_forseth-major-threats-norway`  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1093/icesjms/fsx020  
**Priority questions:** Q9, Q10

## Key findings

- Norwegian expert committee ranks threats on two axes (severity to populations × likelihood of worsening).
- **Escaped farmed salmon** and **salmon lice from fish farms** are placed among **expanding population threats** (severe impact possible and trend worsening).
- Frames lice as **increased mortality of sea-migrating smolts** affecting numerous populations across production areas—not only a minor stressor.

## Evidence type

- review (expert committee classification / semi-quantitative assessment)

## Population-level relevance

- Explicit **population** framing: threat categories include pathways to critically endangered/lost populations vs. reduced returns; lice classified as **population-level** concern in Norway.

## Competing or conflicting points

- Provides the **official Norwegian scientific prioritisation** that motivates strong regulatory attention to lice; contrasts with reviews that rank aquaculture-related lice lower where farm proximity is minimal (e.g. English context in Gillson et al. 2022).

## Uncertainty / limitations

- Expert-elicitation-based; not a formal meta-analysis of effect sizes; Norway-specific.

## Context

- Geography: Norway
- Life stage: sea-migrating smolts (among other life-history context in review)
- System type: open-net pen aquaculture (as threat source); wild populations in rivers/coastal migration
- Time period: assessment contemporary to publication (~2017); committee context pre-/early TLS era
