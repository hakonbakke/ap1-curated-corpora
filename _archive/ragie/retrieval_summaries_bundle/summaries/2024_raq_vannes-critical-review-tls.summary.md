# Salmon lice biology, environmental factors, and smolt behaviour with implications for the Norwegian salmon farming management system: A critical review

**Document ID:** `2024_raq_vannes-critical-review-tls`  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1111/raq.12953  
**Priority questions:** Q1, Q2, Q5, Q7, Q8, Q10  
**Provenance / COI:** Industry-funded programme; first author **Nova Sea** board membership—corpus flags potential **one-sided** emphasis on TLS **overestimation** (see `metadata.yaml` `coi_notes`).

## Key findings

- Argues TLS **overestimates** lice-induced mortality via combined biases: **migration timing/duration** (telemetry: earlier/shorter vs common TLS assumptions), **larval production** neglect of damping factors, **calibration** issues (sentinel cages: fish ~4–5× larger than wild smolts; sea trout proxy limits), **post-attachment lice loss** understated for several endpoints, and **farm lice vs population status** weak association in their analysis framing.

## Evidence type

- review / critique

## Population-level relevance

- Targets **inference from individual/monitoring scale to management classifications** affecting **population-relevant capacity regulation**; claims farm signal **not clearly** linked to **population outcomes** in their reading—**contested** by EG and by Jansen et al. (2025).

## Competing or conflicting points

- **Central antagonist paper** to TLS expert-group position; cites **Eliasen et al. (2021)**; triggers **Stige et al. (2025)** comment and **Van Nes et al. (2025)** response; **Jansen et al. (2025)** presented as partial counter-evidence at returns scale.

## Uncertainty / limitations

- Telemetry evidence concentrated in **limited rivers/years** (western Norway); acknowledges asymmetric treatment of under- vs over-estimation factors per critics.

## Context

- Geography: Norway (TLS)
- Life stage: smolt migration / post-smolt exposure
- System type: open-net pen salmon farming + regulatory TLS
